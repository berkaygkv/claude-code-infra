## Summary

The Turkish real estate market is in a correction phase with declining real prices and suppressed credit-based demand due to tight monetary policy. For cash-rich long-term investors, the current environment may offer entry at discounted real-term valuations. For credit-dependent buyers, conditions remain unfavorable and are not expected to improve before late 2026. The market's trajectory is heavily tied to future interest rate decisions and potential capital rotation from gold into real estate.

### Homeownership Trends

16. **Homeownership rate:** Rose from 56.1% (2024) to projected 57.1% (2025). Tenancy rate fell from 28.0% to 27.0%. Interpretation: landlords may be selling to their tenants. (07:04-07:10 -- SHOWN/INFERRED)

### Credit & Financing

17. **Interest rate threshold:** Below 2% monthly is the critical trigger -- at that level, ~50% of potential buyers would consider loans. (13:15 -- STATED)
18. **Halkbank CEO forecast:** Housing loan rates expected to drop significantly in H2 2026, potentially below 2% monthly. (11:30 -- STATED)
19. **Gold as financing source:** 69% of homebuyers either sold gold or borrowed against gold to finance purchases. (18:07 -- STATED/SHOWN)
20. **Savings finance companies ("Evim"):** Account for ~5,000 housing sales/month, inflating official "mortgaged sales" figures. (19:10 -- STATED)

### Regulatory Environment

21. **LTV ratios (BRSA macroprudential rules):**
    - First-time buyers: up to 90% LTV for homes under 5M TRY, decreasing for pricier homes.
    - Second-home buyers: drastically lower -- 22.5% LTV under 5M TRY, down to 5% for homes over 20M TRY. (13:43 -- SHOWN)
22. **Property tax revaluation:** Capped at max 3x previous value. May have incentivized some 2025 transactions to lock in lower title deed fees. (08:56-09:17 -- STATED/INFERRED)
23. **Citizenship-by-investment:** Not mentioned in the video.

### Capital Flows

24. **Outbound > Inbound:** Turkish citizens now invest more in overseas real estate than foreigners invest in Turkey, creating a current account deficit in this item. (08:04 -- STATED)

## Hedging & Uncertainty Signals

- Buyukduman called his interpretation of 2023 sales figures a "theory" and "intuitive," explicitly stating he "cannot prove it" (06:41 -- STATED). This marks his demand-pull-forward thesis as speculative.
- Interest rate forecasts for H2 2026 are contingent on economic program success and political stability -- not guaranteed.

## Bottom Line Assessment

**For cash buyers with long-term horizon:** The current correction offers entry at lower real-term valuations. The 69% gold-financing statistic suggests a massive potential demand wave if gold prices stabilize and capital rotates into real estate. This is a contrarian entry window.

**For credit-dependent buyers:** Wait. The 2% monthly rate threshold is the key trigger, and credible forecasts place that in H2 2026 at the earliest. Current LTV restrictions for second-home buyers (5-22.5%) make leveraged investment nearly impractical.

**For rental yield investors:** Rental growth is decelerating below inflation. Unless buying at sufficiently discounted prices, rental yields will compress further through 2026.

**Strength of evidence:** MODERATE. The analysis relies on one expert's interpretation of IGD data and Halkbank CEO forecasts. No competing viewpoints or bearish scenarios were presented. The speaker made at least one factual error (2023 sales figure), suggesting some claims deserve independent verification.

**Key uncertainty:** Everything hinges on the CBRT's rate path. If rate cuts are delayed beyond H2 2026 (election dynamics, inflation persistence), the "wait" period extends significantly.
